RATAN TATA

A VISIONARY INDUSTRIALIST AND PHILANTHROPIST

About

Ratan Naval Tata, born on December 28, 1937, is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. He led the Tata Group from 1991 to 2012, and again as interim chairman in 2016–17. Under his leadership, Tata Group grew into a global brand, acquiring major companies like Jaguar Land Rover and Tetley.Ratan Tata has passed away at the age of 86. His death was confirmed on October 9, 2024. Under Ratan Tata’s leadership, 65% of Tata Group’s profits were directed to charitable causes, supporting education, healthcare, scientific research, and countless other initiatives designed to improve lives. This was not simply a corporate policy—it reflected Tata’s deep belief that the success of any organisation should be measured by how much it contributes to society. By March 2024, Tata Group’s total donations had reached $102 billion, an extraordinary sum that mirrors the family’s commitment to making a positive difference in the world.

Key Achievements

  • Chairman of Tata Group (1991–2012, 2016–17)
  • Instrumental in Tata's acquisition of global brands (e.g., JLR, Tetley)
  • Introduced the Tata Nano – the world’s cheapest car
  • Recipient of Padma Bhushan (2000) and Padma Vibhushan (2008)
  • Active philanthropist and supporter of innovation
